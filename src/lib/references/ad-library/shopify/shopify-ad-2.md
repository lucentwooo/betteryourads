# Shopify — Ad 2: Olajuwon Ajanaku / Eastside Golf Testimonial

**Image:** ./shopify-ad-2.jpg
**Awareness Stage:** Solution Aware
**How to recognize this stage:** Uses founder social proof ("If you're doing it the right way, you're on Shopify") from a real brand founder (Olajuwon Ajanaku, Eastside Golf). Targets people who have a business / brand concept and are comparing e-comm platforms. The testimonial is the differentiator, not a feature or price.

## The Angle
Peer validation / "right way" positioning. Using a founder quote with the phrase "the right way" positions Shopify not just as an option, but as the *correct* option — other platforms are implicitly the wrong way. The angle: *the founders you admire are on Shopify. If you're serious, so should you.*

## The Concept
Collage of Eastside Golf product shots + brand artwork floating at slight angles around a center hero image of Olajuwon (the founder) holding up a lavender sweatshirt with the brand's golfer graphic. Quote sits at the top, attribution below, Shopify logo bottom-right. Insight: for platform/tool brands, don't show your product — show your *customers' products*. Shopify is invisible; Eastside Golf is visible.

## Copy Breakdown
- **Headline (quote):** "If you're doing it the right way, you're on Shopify." — Story-First framework with authority framing. "The right way" is the key phrase — it's prescriptive, not descriptive. Not "I love Shopify" but "this is how you should do it." Very confident language from a founder who has earned the right to be prescriptive.
- **Attribution:** "Olajuwon Ajanaku / Founder - Eastside Golf" — sans-serif, two-line. "Founder" specifically chosen over "CEO" or "Co-founder" — implies he built it himself.
- **Brand mark:** Shopify bag + wordmark bottom-right.
- **Hook mechanism:** Authoritative quote + visible product work. The quote promises "right way"; the surrounding collage shows what right looks like.

## Design Analysis
- **Composition:** 9:16 vertical. Top 25% quote + attribution on light gray canvas. Middle 60% product collage — hero photograph of Olajuwon + sweatshirt with 3-4 floating supporting images (Eastside Golf brand artwork of a Black golfer figure, product shots of hangers, founder portrait in crowd). Bottom 15% Shopify logo + negative space.
- **Typography:** Editorial sans-serif (Shopify Sans or similar) for the quote — medium weight, regular case (not all-caps). Quote marks in matching gray. Attribution in smaller weight, tighter tracking. Feels like a New York Times business profile pull-quote.
- **Color system:** Light gray background (~#EDEDED — slightly warmer than pure white), dark gray/near-black quote text, natural photo colors in the collage (lavender sweatshirt, cream/white jackets, khaki pants, pastel artwork). Shopify green logo as tiny accent. The collage lets the customer brand's colors dominate.
- **Imagery:** Five layered images in loose collage arrangement. Hero image (Olajuwon + sweatshirt) is center/largest. Supporting images are at varied angles (-5 to +5 degrees), slight overlap, subtle shadow giving them paper-cutout depth. The collage style mimics a lookbook or pinboard, not a product catalog.
- **Grid / layout:** Paper-cutout collage with quote overlay. Relaxed editorial — no strict grid.
- **Brand register:** Editorial / Creator-business. Closest analog: Fast Company profile layouts, Business of Fashion founder features, Crew Co retail lookbooks.

## Why It Converts
1. **"The right way" is authoritative positioning.** More compelling than any benefit statement because it's prescriptive. It makes competitor platforms feel like the "wrong way."
2. **Real Black founder, real brand (Eastside Golf).** Representation + credibility. Eastside Golf is a real, respected brand — founder is real, business is visible, success is implied.
3. **Collage style = behind-the-scenes feel.** Looks like a founder's moodboard or Instagram carousel, not a designed ad.
4. **Shopify is invisible in the ad but everywhere in the story.** You see Eastside Golf's products, art, and founder — Shopify is the empty space behind it all. Implicit rather than explicit = more trustworthy.
5. **Hero gesture (holding up the product) is triumphant.** The founder presenting his product is an archetypal "I made this" gesture. Aspirational for would-be founders.

## Why It Looks Premium
- Loose collage layout avoids the "designed ad" feel.
- Editorial type treatment (quote marks, attribution) elevates the testimonial from "review" to "feature article."
- Light gray background is a considered choice (warmer than white, cooler than cream).
- Subtle shadows on collage images give them depth without heaviness.
- Customer brand visibility > Shopify brand visibility.

## Creative Director Lens
This is elite-tier B2B/platform brand work. The decision to center a Black founder (Olajuwon) running a golf brand (a traditionally white/country-club space) is editorially strong — it tells the viewer Shopify is for founders *creating new spaces*, not copying old ones. The collage treatment is deliberately imperfect — slight angles, hand-placed feel — which humanizes what could have been a corporate testimonial. A CD would celebrate the casting and the collage both. Minor critique: the quote itself is slightly vague — "the right way" is strong but unbacked. A quote with one more specific detail ("Shopify made [X] possible") would be even stronger. Also: the Shopify logo at the bottom-right could integrate more elegantly — right now it sits slightly orphaned from the composition. Still, this is a top 5% platform-brand ad for B2B confidence.

## Media Buyer Lens
This is a **mid-funnel / brand-lift ad** for Shopify's entrepreneur ICP — specifically people who are platform-comparing (Shopify vs. BigCommerce vs. Squarespace vs. WooCommerce). Audiences: competitor app users, DTC brand founders, creator commerce interest, Kickstarter/Etsy seller lookalikes. CPM: $15-25 (entrepreneur audiences are more expensive). Expected CTR: 0.8-1.4% cold, 1.5-2.2% on warm audiences. This isn't a direct-response unit — it builds the "Shopify is the serious choice" association over 2-3 exposures before conversion ads close. Fatigue is slow (~3 weeks) because the creative is editorial and narrative, not offer-based. Weakness: on mobile at Reels thumbnail scale, the collage gets busy — a tighter crop or fewer collage elements would help.

## Transferable Patterns
- **"If you're doing it the right way, you're on [brand]."** prescriptive positioning template. Works when you have earned authority in your category.
- **Customer product > your product in the visual.** For platform/tool/SaaS brands, show the customer's work. You become the empty space behind their success.
- **Collage / moodboard layout for founder testimonials.** Multiple images at slight angles feel more authentic than a single hero shot.
- **Light gray canvas (not white).** Softer than white, more editorial than cream. Magazine-pull-quote register.
- **Hero gesture + supporting collage.** Biggest image shows the moment of founder pride; smaller images show the ecosystem.
